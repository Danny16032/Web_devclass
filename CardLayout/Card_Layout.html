Koenigsegg Jesko Absolut

The Koenigsegg Jesko Absolut is the high-speed, low-drag variant of the Jesko, designed specifically for ultimate speed records with a streamlined, "stealthy" body and extended rear, eliminating the need for a large rear wing. It features a 1,600-horsepower, 5.0L twin-turbo V8 engine, a 9-speed multi-clutch transmission, and a low drag coefficient of 0.278 Cd, with theoretical top speeds exceeding 300 mph.

Pagani Huayra

The Pagani Huayra is an Italian-made, mid-engine hypercar developed by Pagani Automobili, succeeding the Zonda and named after the Quechua wind god. Powered by a Mercedes-AMG twin-turbo V12, it is renowned for its exquisite craftsmanship, lightweight carbo-titanium chassis, active aerodynamics, and bespoke, handcrafted interior. The Huayra was first unveiled at the 2011 Geneva Motor Show, and the company is known for producing exclusive, limited-run models such as the Roadster and the track-focused BC variant.
