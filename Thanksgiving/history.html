Origins of the Holiday

Thanksgiving is a long-standing American holiday rooted in gratitude, harvest traditions, and cultural exchange. Its earliest form dates back to the early 17th century.

The 1621 Harvest Feast

The first Thanksgiving is commonly traced to a 1621 harvest feast shared between the Pilgrims and the Wampanoag people. It was a three-day celebration of cooperation and survival.

Becoming a National Holiday

Although Thanksgiving was celebrated at various times for centuries, it wasnt declared a national holiday until 1863, during the presidency of Abraham Lincoln.

Modern Traditions

Today, Thanksgiving traditions include feasting, parades, family gatherings, and reflections on gratitude. It remains a central piece of American culture.
